Robotic Arm – MIE243 Final Project

Course project • Robotic joints & power transmission
SolidWorks

Designed a 2-DOF differential joint and a 1-DOF worm-gear rotation joint for a robotic arm. The differential joint is rigidly connected to a planetary gearbox to provide 3-DOF motion at the base, while keeping the assembly compact and efficient for torque distribution.

The worm-gear rotation joint drives belts and is intentionally hard to backdrive, eliminating the need for extra brakes and improving stability when the arm is holding a static pose under load. Joint concepts were selected using a multivoting process that balanced kinematics, integration, and feasibility.

Differential gear design Worm gear + belts Load paths & stiffness Multivoting selection

Carbon-Fibre Front Bumper

Personal project • Composite automotive component
Independent

Fabricated a carbon-fibre front bumper for a Toyota MR2 Spyder, going through the full composite workflow: mold preparation, tooling gel coat, fiberglass backing, carbon-fibre layup, vacuum bagging, curing, trimming, and finishing.

Iterated on vacuum bagging technique to avoid leaks, wrinkles, and voids, learning how fibre orientation, resin distribution, and pressure control affect surface quality and laminate integrity.

Mold design & prep Carbon layup Vacuum bagging Automotive fitment

Tourbillon Watch Mechanism

Independent project • Precision mechanism design
Inventor

Modelled a tourbillon watch movement in CAD, including the Swiss lever escapement, balance assembly, and high-ratio gear train. Used gear standards and escapement geometry references to keep the design physically plausible rather than purely aesthetic.

Explored how small changes in angles, clearances, and tooth counts affect locking, impulse, and overall running behaviour, building intuition for precision tolerances and manufacturability at watch scales.

Swiss lever escapement High-ratio gear trains Balance & inertia Precision tolerances

Sleep Well, Age Well – Sleep Tracking Pajamas

APS112 – Engineering Strategies & Practice II • Team of five
Concept design

Developed a concept for sleep tracking pajamas for retired adults in the GTA as part of a full Conceptual Design Specification (CDS). The design embeds non-obtrusive sensors into a familiar garment, paired with a removable electronics module to track sleep without extra devices.

Worked through the full ESP framework: translating client statements into engineering requirements, mapping stakeholders and the service environment, and using FOC (Functions–Objectives–Constraints) diagrams and decision matrices to converge on the final concept.

Requirements development Wearable design Stakeholder analysis Conceptual design

Scroll Score – Android Scroll Tracking App

Independent project • Android & accessibility
Android

Built Scroll Score, an Android app that uses an Accessibility Service to detect scroll events in short-form content apps (like TikTok and YouTube Shorts) and logs per-app scroll counts and usage over time.

Implemented debouncing and event filtering so each scroll gesture translates into a meaningful unit of “attention spent,” with simple UI for daily totals, app breakdowns, and exportable history for offline analysis.

Kotlin AccessibilityService Usage analytics CSV export
